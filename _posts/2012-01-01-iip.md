	<h1>India's industrial output growth drops to -4.3% in Sep '19: CSO data
	</h1>	

	<p>India's factory output grew by -4.3% in Sep '19, down from -1.4% last month, according to new data released by the Central Statistics Office. In the same month last year, Sep '18, the output grew by 4.6%.  The slowdown comes in large part due to decline in production of Capital goods (-20.7%) and Consumer durables(-9.9%). </p>

    <p>The latest number is the worst performance of the IIP index since the series began in April 2012, a 89-month low.</p>
    
	<p><iframe src="https://embed.loki.ai/state_of_economy/iip/General.html" style="width: 100%; height: 400px; border: 0px; overflow: hidden;" scrolling="no" scroll="no"></iframe></p>

    <p>Manufacturing, which contributes over 77% to the IIP figures, shrank by -3.9% compared to 4.8% in the same month of last year.  Mining contributes around 14%, and shrank by -8.5% as against 0.1% in the same month last year, and the electricity sector (8% of IIP) shrank by -2.6% compared to 8.2% same month last year.</p>
	<p>17 of the 23 industry groups included in the index were in the red--meaning negative growth--in Sep '19 compared to the corresponding month last year.</p>
	
	<p><iframe src="https://embed.loki.ai/state_of_economy/iip/industry_3m.html" style="width: 100%; height: 400px; border: 0px; overflow: hidden;" scrolling="no" scroll="no"></iframe></p>
	
	<p>The factory output is measured by the Index of Industrial Production (IIP)-a composite index that measures year-on-year change in the volume of production of selected industrial goods, with respect to a base period, 2011-12 in this case. The index is used to measure the level of monthly industrial activity in the Indian economy and forms a key input in estimating the Gross Value Added of the manufacturing sector for quarterly GDP calculations.</p>
